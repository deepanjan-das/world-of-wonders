What is a World Heritage site?

According to the Wikipedia, "A UNESCO World Heritage Site is a place that is listed by UNESCO as of special cultural or physical significance."

The sites are chosen according to their outstanding cultural or natural importance to the common heritage of humanity. The list further enables the international community to preserve these sites.

The list is created and maintained by the international World Heritage Programme administered by the UNESCO World Heritage Committee, composed of representatives of 21 states, elected by their General Assembly.

How is a cultural World Heritage Site chosen?

There are certain criteria that a particular site has to satisfy in order to be designated as a cultural heritage by the UNESCO -

  • Represents a master of human creative genius
  • Exhibits an important change of human values
  • A unique or exceptional testimony to a cultural tradition/civilization which is living or which has disappeared
  • An outstanding example of a type of structure that illustrates a significant stage in human history
  • An outstanding example of a traditional human settlement
  • Is directly or tangibly associated with events or living traditions
How is a natural World Heritage Site chosen?

The following are the criteria for designating a particular site as a natural heritage -

  • A superlative natural phenomena or areas of exceptional natural beauty
  • An outstanding example representing major stages of the earth's history
  • An outstanding example representing significant on-going ecological and biological processes
  • One of the most important and significant natural for in-situ conservation of biological diversity
